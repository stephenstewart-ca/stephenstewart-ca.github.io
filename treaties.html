Termination, in this context, means ending all pre-existing sovereign status through coercion into land claims and self-government agreements that convert First Nations into municipalities, reserves into fee simple lands and extinguish all inherent, aboriginal and treaty rights.

This termination policy, practiced by @JoeWild_TAG of @GCIndigenous, isn't just deprecated but expressly disclaimed by the Constitution of Canada that conversely recognizes and affirms the aboriginal and treaty rights of the aboriginal peoples of Canada. The Constitution is the foundation for all the laws of Canada, the standard every law conforms to. Amending the Constitution would require the consent of all of the parties, presumably at a Constitutional conference.
